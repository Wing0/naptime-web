Privacy Policy — Naptime Free

Effective Date: June 14, 2026

This policy applies only to the free, donation-supported edition of Naptime ("Naptime Free"). If you use the paid edition of Naptime, that app collects and transmits no data and is covered by our standard Privacy Policy instead.

Naptime Free is offered to you at no cost. In exchange, you agree to contribute anonymous data about how you sleep, which we are free to use as described below. This is the trade that keeps the free edition free. This policy explains, in plain terms, exactly what we collect, how we keep it anonymous, what we do with it, and how you can take it all back at any time.

1. Your Consent

When you first open Naptime Free, you are shown a screen explaining what the app collects, that the data is anonymous, and that it may be shared with research partners and other third parties. Agreeing to these data-donation terms is required to use the free edition — no data is uploaded until you have agreed, but the free edition cannot be used without agreeing. If you prefer not to share your data, you can use the paid edition of Naptime instead, which keeps all session data on your device and uploads nothing.

Using Naptime Free with data contribution enabled constitutes your agreement that we may collect, store, analyze, and otherwise exploit the anonymized data described in this policy without restriction and without further compensation to you, for as long as it remains in our systems.

2. What We Collect

Sleep & Nap Session Data

When you complete a nap or an overnight sleep session and have consented, the app uploads a summary of that session. This includes:

  • Timing: session start and wake-up times, total duration, the length and position of your longest continuous sleep, and any sleep/wake segments detected.
  • Movement statistics: aggregated, time-windowed summaries of your phone's motion sensor readings (e.g. high/low/median movement per minute, percentiles, burst scores). We do not upload raw, continuous accelerometer streams — only these derived statistics.
  • Sleep quality signals: detected wake events, micro-arousals/twitches, sleep efficiency, snooze events, and any star/emoji rating or manual corrections you make to a session.
  • Session settings snapshot: the relevant app settings in effect for that session (e.g. alarm sound, sensitivity, nap duration, wake-up time, tracking toggles, Do Not Disturb status).
  • Device metadata: your device manufacturer, model, Android version, and SDK level, plus whether the build was a debug build.

Stability & Crash Data

To keep the app reliable, we upload technical records of crashes and abnormal process exits (e.g. the reason the app was terminated, memory usage at the time, and whether a session was active). These contain no personal content from your sessions beyond the device metadata listed above.

What We Do NOT Collect

  • No account, name, email address, or phone number.
  • No advertising ID, Google account, or persistent personal identifier.
  • No location data, contacts, microphone audio, or content from other apps.
  • No raw accelerometer recordings.
  • No third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs of any kind (no Firebase, Google Analytics, crash-reporting SDKs, etc.). All uploads go only to our own servers.

3. How We Keep It Anonymous

Naptime Free is designed so that the data we receive cannot be traced back to you as an individual:

  • No identity is ever required or collected. You never create an account, and we never ask for anything that identifies you personally.
  • Data is keyed only by a random installation identifier. The first time data is uploaded, the app generates a random identifier (a UUID) for that installation. This number is not derived from your hardware, your Google account, or any advertising ID, and it is generated freshly if you reinstall or reset your data. It lets us group sessions from the same installation (for example, to measure whether sleep improves over time) without knowing who you are.
  • No cross-referencing. Because we hold no name, email, or device fingerprint, we have no practical means of linking a random installation identifier to a real person.

The combination of these measures means the dataset we hold is anonymous: it describes how sleep happens, not who slept.

4. How We Use the Data — and Our Right to Exploit It

In exchange for providing Naptime Free at no charge, you grant us a worldwide, royalty-free, perpetual, and irrevocable right to use the anonymized and aggregated data we collect for any lawful purpose. This expressly includes, without limitation:

  • Improving Naptime's sleep-detection algorithms, features, and reliability.
  • Performing research and statistical analysis on sleep and rest.
  • Building, training, and validating models and other products derived from the data.
  • Producing and publishing aggregated insights, benchmarks, and reports.
  • Sharing, licensing, selling, or otherwise commercializing the anonymized and aggregated data with third parties, including partners and researchers.

Because the data is anonymous, "your" contribution becomes part of a pooled dataset that we are free to exploit as described. This right survives your deletion of the app, except with respect to data you retract under Section 6 before it has been irretrievably aggregated.

5. Where the Data Is Stored & Who Processes It

Uploaded data is transmitted over an encrypted (HTTPS) connection and stored on servers we operate. Each upload is cryptographically signed to ensure it genuinely came from the Naptime app and was not tampered with in transit.

Our servers are hosted in European data centres operated by Hetzner Online GmbH, which acts as our hosting provider. Hetzner stores the data on our behalf but has no independent right to use it. Because the data we collect is anonymous and we retain unrestricted rights over it, no further data processors need to be named for the purposes of using this data.

6. Your Right to Retract Your Data

Even though the data is anonymous, you remain fully in control and can withdraw it at any time. You have two independent ways to do this:

  • From your device: Uninstalling the app, or tapping "Delete my data" in the app's settings, permanently erases all sleep records, session history, crash logs, consent state, and the random installation identifier from your device.
  • From our servers: The same "Delete my data" action also sends a deletion request to our servers, which permanently and irreversibly removes all sessions, crash logs, and metadata associated with your installation identifier. Once deleted, this data cannot be re-linked or recovered.

Using "Delete my data" is also how you stop future contributions: it clears your consent, so the app stops uploading and will ask for your consent again before any further data is contributed. Note that data which has already been irreversibly aggregated or anonymized into a pooled dataset before your retraction may no longer be separable, and to that extent cannot be individually withdrawn.

If you would like assistance retracting your data, contact us using the details below.

7. Data Sharing

We do not sell or share information that identifies you, because we never collect any. We may freely share, license, or sell the anonymized and aggregated data as described in Section 4.

8. Children's Privacy

Naptime Free is not directed to children under the age of digital consent in their jurisdiction. We do not knowingly collect data from such children. Because we collect no identifying information, we cannot determine a user's age; if you believe a child has used the app, you may retract the associated data as described in Section 6.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new policy on this page and updating the effective date above.
